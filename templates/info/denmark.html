Denmark

About Denmark

Denmark, a Scandinavian country in Northern Europe, is known for its flat landscapes, extensive coastlines, and commitment to sustainability. The country is made up of the Jutland Peninsula and numerous islands, with a strong focus on renewable energy and environmental protection. Denmark's forests, wetlands, and coastal areas are home to a variety of plant and animal species.

Vegetation in Denmark

Denmark's vegetation includes a mix of deciduous forests, grasslands, and coastal plants. Key species include:

  • Beech Trees
  • Oak Trees
  • Birch Trees
  • Pine Trees
  • Spruce Trees
  • Heather
  • Sea Buckthorn
  • Reeds (in wetlands)
