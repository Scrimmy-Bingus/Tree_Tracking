Brazil

About Brazil

Brazil, the largest country in South America, is renowned for its vast Amazon Rainforest, diverse ecosystems, and rich biodiversity. It is home to the Amazon River, the world's largest river by discharge volume, and the Pantanal, the world's largest tropical wetland. Brazil's ecosystems are critical to global climate regulation and biodiversity conservation.

Vegetation in Brazil

Brazil's vegetation is incredibly diverse, ranging from tropical rainforests to savannas and mangroves. Key species include:

  • Brazil Nut Tree
  • Rubber Tree
  • Pau Brasil (Brazilwood)
  • Açaí Palm
  • Mangrove Trees
  • Ipê (Trumpet Trees)
  • Jatobá
  • Buriti Palm
