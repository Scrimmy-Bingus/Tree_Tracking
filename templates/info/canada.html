About Canada

Canada, the second-largest country in the world, is known for its vast wilderness, diverse ecosystems, and abundant natural resources. From the Rocky Mountains to the boreal forests and the Arctic tundra, Canada's landscapes are home to a wide variety of wildlife, including bears, moose, and beavers. The country is also a global leader in environmental conservation and sustainable practices.

Vegetation in Canada

Canada's vegetation is diverse, ranging from dense forests to Arctic tundra. Key species include:

  • Maple Trees
  • Spruce Trees
  • Pine Trees
  • Fir Trees
  • Birch Trees
  • Cedar Trees
  • Wildflowers (e.g., Fireweed)
  • Arctic Moss
