About Belgium

Belgium, a small yet diverse country in Western Europe, is known for its rich history, medieval towns, and vibrant culture. Despite its small size, Belgium boasts a variety of landscapes, including dense forests, rolling hills, and coastal plains. The country is committed to environmental sustainability and has several initiatives to protect its natural habitats and biodiversity.

Vegetation in Belgium

Belgium's vegetation includes a mix of deciduous and coniferous forests, as well as grasslands and wetlands. Key species include:

  • Beech Trees
  • Oak Trees
  • Birch Trees
  • Pine Trees
  • Spruce Trees
  • Wildflowers (e.g., Bluebells)
  • Heather
  • Reeds (in wetlands)
